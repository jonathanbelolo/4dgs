# Document 3: Corporate Strategy & Revenue Engineering

**Title:** *The Dual-Entity Framework: vZero Publisher vs. vZero Retail*
**Classification:** *Strategic Business Plan*

## I. Structural Overview: The "Hedged Garden"

vZero operates as two legally and financially distinct entities under a single Holding Company (vZero Holdings). This protects the most valuable assets—the **Neural IP**—from the operational liabilities of physical locations.

### 1. vZero Publisher (The "Alpha" Entity)

The Publisher is an **Intellectual Property (IP) Powerhouse**. Its value is derived from its library of volumetric "Masters" and its proprietary capture technology.

* **The $10M Show Cap:** We budget $10M per AAA production. This includes a $5M-$6M recoupable advance for the Artist and $4M for the 4DGS capture and "Neural World" world-building.
* **Ownership:** The Publisher owns 100% of the raw volumetric data and the distribution rights for LBE, Home, and Mobile.

### 2. vZero Retail (The "Beta" Entity)

The Retailer is a **Technology Infrastructure Operator**. Its value is derived from its prime real estate and "Reference Class" hardware.

* **Function:** Operates the Flagships (Vegas, London, etc.).
* **The Strategic Hedge:** Retail is not "content-locked." It is a multi-purpose sensorium. If the music vertical fluctuates, Retail can immediately pivot to hosting high-fee corporate product launches or neural cinema without changing a single piece of hardware.

---

## II. The Revenue Waterfall (Per Ticket)

The financial "heartbeat" of vZero is the **50/50 Revenue Split**. This ensures the Publisher is funded for new content while the Retailer can cover high-prime real estate costs.

**Example: A $100 Ticket at the Vegas Flagship**

* **Gross Revenue:** $100
* **vZero Retail Retention (50%):** $50
* *Used for:* Rent, Staff, Electricity, and Hardware Amortization.
* **Net Retail Margin:** ~$15 - $20 per ticket.


* **vZero Publisher Retention (50%):** $50
* **The 30% Music Royalty (Off Publisher Gross):** $15 (Paid to Label/Artist).
* **Net Publisher Margin:** **$35 per ticket.**



### Why this works:

The Publisher uses that $35/ticket to recoup the $10M production cost. At 300,000 visitors annually across two flagships, the Publisher recoups a $10M show in **under 12 months**—everything after that is pure profit for the IP holders.
