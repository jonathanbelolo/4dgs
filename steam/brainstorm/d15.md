In Years 2 and 3, your funding strategy shifts from "Proving the Concept" to "Financing the Rollout." Because you are building a business that is half-software (Publisher) and half-hardware/real-estate (Retailer), you can access diverse pools of capital that a typical "pure tech" startup cannot.

Here is the strategic funding roadmap for 2028 and 2029.

### Year 2 (2028): The Growth Inflection (Series B)

**Target Raise: $60M – $80M**
By Year 2, you have data from Vegas and London showing high repeat visitation and the successful launch of your 3rd and 4th shows.

* **Venture Capital (Equity):** You will return to top-tier VCs (like Andreessen Horowitz or Sequoia) to fund the **Publisher's** expansion. The goal here is to scale the 4DGS pipeline—moving from 2 shows a year to a more automated process.
* **Strategic Corporate Investment:** This is the year you look for "Smart Money" from the industry.
* **Valve:** A strategic investment from Valve to ensure vZero remains an exclusive anchor for the Steam Frame.
* **Universal Music Group (UMG) or Sony Music:** Labels may invest to secure a "Digital Touring Platform" for their entire roster.


* **Venture Debt:** Since you now have predictable revenue and high-value hardware assets (NVIDIA racks), you can raise **$10M–$15M in Venture Debt**. This is non-dilutive capital (you don't give up shares) used to bridge the gap between flagship openings.

---

### Year 3 (2029): The Global Scale & Infrastructure (Series C)

**Target Raise: $150M – $200M**
In Year 3, the goal is to reach 8+ flagships and 250+ licensed sites. At this stage, you move away from traditional "VC" and toward "Infrastructure Finance."

* **Private Equity (PE):** Large PE firms (like Blackstone or KKR) are interested in the **Retailer** side. They view your flagships like high-yield real estate or "Experiential Anchors" for high-end malls. They can provide the massive capital needed to build out Tokyo, Dubai, and NYC simultaneously.
* **Project Finance & Asset-Backed Lending:** You can borrow against the physical assets of your existing venues to fund new ones. This is the "IMAX model"—using the cash flow of Venue A to pay for the hardware in Venue C.
* **Revenue-Based Financing:** For the **Publisher**, you can "sell" a percentage of future royalties from your 6-show library to specialist funds in exchange for immediate cash. This allows you to fund Show #7 and #8 without further diluting your own equity.
