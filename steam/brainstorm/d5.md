Choosing the right city for a **vZero Flagship** is less about "where the fans are" and more about "where the high-net-worth (HNW) density meets the tech-curious tourism." Given your $100 ticket target and 2027 deployment, you need a city that can support a "luxury entertainment" spend.

Based on 2026 market data and your specific music industry connections, here is the short-list:

### 1. Las Vegas, USA (The Strategic No-Brainer)

* **The Rationale:** Vegas is currently the "Immersive Capital of the World." With the success of the **Sphere**, the audience here is already primed for high-ticket volumetric entertainment.
* **The Capex Advantage:** Las Vegas is optimized for "Mega-LBE." You can secure real estate within **Area15** or near the Sphere where the "Immersive" infrastructure (high-bandwidth power, cooling for GPU racks) is already standard.
* **The Ticket:** People in Vegas don't blink at $100 for a 45-minute premium experience.
* **Why for Valve:** It's the ultimate global showroom. Every tech executive and influencer passes through Vegas annually for CES.

### 2. London, UK (The Music Industry Fortress)

* **The Rationale:** London is the global hub for music IP and tech-creative talent. With the **ABBA Arena** proving that "virtual residency" can run for years, Londoners understand the value of a digital performance.
* **The Capex Advantage:** Access to the world’s best spatial audio engineers and Gaussian Splatting researchers (many based out of UCL or local VFX houses).
* **The Ticket:** A $75–$100 (£60–£80) price point aligns with West End premium theatre and high-end immersive shows like *Frameless*.
* **Why for Valve:** It gives them a massive European foothold in a city that prioritizes "Culture Tech."

### 3. Tokyo, Japan (The Tech-Mecca)

* **The Rationale:** Japan has the highest per-capita engagement with "Virtual Idols" and digital performers. The "Star Factor" you mentioned is magnified 10x in a culture that already treats digital avatars as A-list celebrities.
* **The Capex Advantage:** Tokyo is the leader in high-density LBE (e.g., *teamLab Planets*, *VR Zone Shinjuku*). They have the most advanced building codes for "hundreds of speakers" and complex electrical loads in small footprints.
* **The Ticket:** Tokyo consumers prioritize "Polish" and "Quality." They will pay for the "Breathtaking" visuals you are promising.
