### 2. The Capex Scaling: 1 Room vs. 2 Rooms

Adding a second room increases your Capex by roughly **60%**, not 100%. This is because your most expensive assets—the **Neural Asset Pipeline** (the $5M artist content) and the **Centralized Server Backbone**—are shared or only partially expanded.

### 3. The Operational "Sweet Spot" (The 2-Room Rule)

From a business standpoint, **two rooms is the minimum for a Flagship.**

* **Staggered Starts:** Room A starts at 12:00, Room B starts at 12:15. This allows your reception and "Onboarding/Safety" staff to work continuously rather than standing idle while a session is running.
* **Redundancy:** If a speaker array in Room A has a glitch, you don't shut down your business. You move the VIPs to Room B.
* **OpEx Efficiency:** One "Master Technician" can monitor the GPU racks for both rooms. Two rooms share one manager, one security guard, and one marketing budget.

### 4. ROI Assumption: Vegas/London

* **2-Room Revenue (70% Occupancy):** ~$840,000 per month.
* **Monthly OpEx (Staff, Rent, Power, Licensing):** ~$350,000.
* **Monthly Profit:** ~$490,000.
* **Payback Period:** You recoup your **$21.6M Capex** in roughly **44 months**.

**However**, if your "Star Factor" drives 90% occupancy (common for high-end Vegas residencies), you hit break-even in **under 32 months**.
