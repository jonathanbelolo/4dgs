**Classification:** Creative Brief — Confidential
**Date:** February 2026
**Version:** 1.0

## Overview

This document defines the visual assets required to support the vZero investor pitch. The assets include a series of images and one sizzle reel video (60–90 seconds) that together tell the story of vZero: the experience, the venue, the technology, and the scale.

The visuals must accomplish four things:
1. **Make them feel it** before they understand it
2. **Show it's real** — buildable, premium, credible
3. **Explain the technology** without overwhelming
4. **Convey the scale** — from one room to a global platform

## Visual Style Guidelines

### Overall Tone
- **Premium, not flashy.** Think Apple keynote, not CES booth.
- **Confident restraint.** Let the concept speak; don't oversell with visual noise.
- **Warm technology.** The tech should feel human, not cold. People are always the focus.

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Deep blacks, warm whites, subtle golds
- **Accent:** Volumetric light (soft blue/purple glow from the performer)
- **Avoid:** Neon, overly saturated colors, "gaming" aesthetic

### Lighting
- **Dramatic chiaroscuro** in the experience shots — the performer is the light source
- **Clean and modern** in the venue/architecture shots
- **Warm and natural** in the Miraval/home shots

### People
- **Diverse, aspirational, but grounded.** Not models — real people having a genuine experience.
- **The performer:** Abstract or silhouette initially (avoids IP issues, keeps focus on the medium). If a real artist likeness is needed, placeholder with a recognizable but generic "star" presence.

### Technical Renders
- **The Gaussian Cloud:** Should look beautiful, not clinical. Think data-as-art.
- **The Server Room:** Clean, powerful, slightly awe-inspiring. Not messy or industrial.

## Open Questions

1. **Artist representation:** Do we use an abstract/silhouette performer, or attempt a realistic (but generic) artist likeness? Silhouette is safer for IP and keeps focus on the medium.

2. **Steam Frame depiction:** Do we have access to official renders/photos of the Steam Frame, or do we create a plausible stand-in headset design?

3. **Venue location:** For the flagship exterior, do we render a specific real-world location (Area15, a London site) or a generic "premium entertainment district"?

4. **Music:** Will the video have licensed music, original composition, or a placeholder track for the pitch version?
