# vZero: Content Verticals Beyond Music

**Classification:** Strategic Exploration — Confidential
**Date:** February 2026
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Overview

Music is the launch vertical for vZero. But the venue hardware — 4DGS rendering, WFS audio, haptic floors, untethered VR headsets — is content-agnostic. Once the infrastructure is proven, additional content verticals can leverage the same platform with different audiences, use cases, and revenue models.

This document explores three high-potential verticals beyond music and sports:

1. **Educational & Cultural Experiences** — immersive learning for schools, families, and cultural institutions
2. **Cinematic Immersion** — placing audiences inside iconic scenes from major film franchises
3. **Video Game Immersion** — standing inside beloved game worlds, from Vice City to Hyrule

---

## Vertical 1: Educational & Cultural Experiences

### The Opportunity

Location-based VR education is an established but underutilized category. Existing offerings (National Geographic VR, museum VR exhibits) are typically low-fidelity, passive, and forgettable. vZero's infrastructure enables a fundamentally different proposition: **volumetric presence in places that no longer exist, cannot be visited, or exist at scales humans cannot perceive.**

### Target Audiences

| Audience | Use Case | Timing |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Schools (K-12)** | Field trips, curriculum-aligned experiences | Weekday mornings (off-peak) |
| **Families** | Weekend educational entertainment | Weekends, holidays |
| **Adults / Lifelong learners** | Evening "explorer" sessions | Weekday evenings |
| **Tourists** | Cultural experiences in destination cities | All day (Vegas, London, Paris, Dubai) |

### Content Categories

#### 1. Ancient Worlds
- **The Pyramids of Giza** — Stand inside the Great Pyramid as it was 4,500 years ago. Torchlit corridors. The burial chamber. Hieroglyphics in original paint.
- **The Colosseum** — Rome, 80 AD. The arena floor. The roar of the crowd. A gladiatorial contest unfolds around you.
- **Pompeii** — The day before Vesuvius. Walk the streets, enter the homes, see the frescoes in their original colors.

#### 2. Natural Wonders
- **The Deep Ocean** — Descend to the Mariana Trench. Bioluminescent creatures. The Titanic wreck at 1:1 scale, walking the decks.
- **The Coral Reef** — The Great Barrier Reef before bleaching. Schools of fish. Sharks passing overhead. Marine biology curriculum tie-in.
- **The Amazon Canopy** — 150 feet up. Birds, insects, the ecosystem at work.

#### 3. Space & Cosmos
- **The Surface of Mars** — Jezero Crater. Perseverance rover beside you. The salmon sky.
- **The International Space Station** — Zero-G. Earth through the cupola. Astronaut daily life.
- **The Scale of the Universe** — Zoom from subatomic to galactic. Powers of ten, experienced viscerally.

#### 4. Cultural Heritage
- **The Louvre (1793)** — The museum as it opened. Napoleon's collection. The Mona Lisa in its original context.
- **The Library of Alexandria** — The ancient world's repository of knowledge, reconstructed from historical records.
- **Notre-Dame (Pre-Fire)** — The cathedral as it stood for 800 years, with the original spire.

### Production Model

Unlike AAA music spectacles, educational content can be produced at lower cost using:

- **Photogrammetry + 4DGS hybrid** — Real-world scans enhanced with volumetric elements (people, animals, dynamic objects)
- **Historical reconstruction** — CG environments based on archaeological and historical research
- **Partnerships** — Museums, universities, cultural institutions contribute research and lend credibility; vZero provides the platform

| Content Type | Estimated Cost | Runtime |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Ancient world (single location) | $1M–$2M | 15–20 min |
| Natural wonder (documentary-style) | $500K–$1M | 10–15 min |
| Space / cosmos | $800K–$1.5M | 15–20 min |
| Cultural heritage | $1M–$2M | 15–20 min |

### Revenue Model

| Channel | Price Point | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **Standard ticket** | $80 | Lower than music spectacles — no artist royalties to pay |
| **School group booking** | $60/student | Institutional pricing for group bookings (10+ students) |
| **Neural Pass inclusion** | Bundled | Part of the subscriber library |
| **Institutional licensing** | $50K–$200K/year | Museums, science centers license content for their own VR installations |

**Pricing rationale:** Educational and cultural content has no artist advance or royalty costs, allowing a lower price point while maintaining strong margins. At $80 standard / $60 for schools, vZero remains 50%+ above Sandbox VR ($36–55) and 2–3x above museum VR exhibits ($15–25). This is still a premium experience — but accessible enough to attract school budgets and fill weekday morning capacity with institutional revenue.

### Strategic Value

- **Fills off-peak capacity.** Weekday mornings are slower for music spectacles. Educational content brings school groups and cultural tourists during these hours — at full price.
- **Expands demographic reach.** Music skews 18–45. Educational content brings children, families, and older adults.
- **Institutional credibility.** Partnerships with the Smithsonian, British Museum, or NASA elevate the brand beyond entertainment.
- **Evergreen content.** The Pyramids don't go out of style. Educational content has a longer shelf life than music releases.

## Vertical 2: Cinematic Immersion

### The Opportunity

Major film franchises generate billions in theatrical, streaming, and merchandise revenue — but the experience ends when the credits roll. Fans can watch the films, buy the toys, visit the theme parks. But they cannot **be inside the scene.**

vZero can offer exactly that: **volumetric presence inside iconic moments from beloved films.**

### The VFX Pipeline Advantage

Modern blockbusters are already produced in fully 3D environments. VFX studios create:

- Complete digital environments (cities, landscapes, interiors)
- Fully rigged digital characters (for wide shots, stunts, augmentation)
- Volumetric captures of actors (for face replacement, digital doubles)

This means the raw material for vZero experiences **already exists** in the archives of studios like:

- **DNEG** (Dune, Dune: Part Two, Inception, Interstellar)
- **Wētā FX** (Lord of the Rings, Avatar, Planet of the Apes)
- **ILM** (Star Wars, Marvel, Jurassic Park)
- **Framestore** (Gravity, Blade Runner 2049, Paddington)

For these studios, repackaging existing assets into a vZero experience is a **new revenue stream from sunk costs.**

### Example Experiences

| Film | Scene | Experience |
|------|-------|------------|
| **Dune: Part Two** | The final duel between Paul Atreides and Feyd-Rautha | Stand in the arena on Arrakis. The knife fight unfolds 10 feet away. The crowd roars. |
| **The Lord of the Rings** | The charge of the Rohirrim at Pelennor Fields | You're on the battlefield as 6,000 horses crest the hill. Théoden's speech. The impact. |
| **The Matrix** | The lobby shootout | Columns shatter around you. Neo and Trinity move in slow motion. Bullet trails freeze in the air. |
| **Interstellar** | Docking with the Endurance | The spin. The silence of space. The tension of the impossible maneuver. |
| **Avatar** | First flight on an Ikran | The cliffs of Pandora. The drop. The bioluminescent forest below. |
| **Jurassic Park** | The T-Rex paddock attack | Rain. The flashlight. The footsteps in the water. The eye at the window. |
| **Blade Runner 2049** | The Wallace Corporation | The golden light. The water. Niander Wallace emerges from the shadows. |
| **Star Wars** | The trench run | X-Wing cockpit. The Death Star surface rushing past. "Use the Force, Luke." |

### Production Model

| Approach | Cost | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Asset licensing from VFX studio** | $500K–$2M | Existing 3D environments, characters, animation |
| **4DGS conversion + optimization** | $300K–$500K | Reformatting for real-time volumetric rendering |
| **Interactive layer + spatial audio** | $200K–$400K | Audience positioning, WFS mix, haptic score |
| **Studio approval + QC** | Variable | Rights holders require creative approval |
| **Total per scene** | **$1M–$3M** | Significantly less than original VFX budget |

### Rights & Licensing

This vertical requires partnerships with:

1. **Film studios** (Warner Bros., Universal, Disney, Legendary, etc.) — IP owners
2. **VFX houses** (DNEG, Wētā, ILM, Framestore) — Asset owners and conversion partners

**Licensing structure options:**

| Model | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Revenue share** | Studio receives 20–30% of ticket/subscription revenue attributed to their IP |
| **Flat license fee** | One-time or annual fee for venue + home rights |
| **Co-production** | Studio invests in production; shared ownership of the vZero experience |

**Why studios would participate:**

- **New revenue from existing assets.** VFX archives are sunk costs sitting on hard drives.
- **Franchise extension.** Keeps IP relevant between theatrical releases.
- **Premium positioning.** vZero is not a theme park ride — it's a prestige experience.
- **Home distribution.** Neural Pass subscribers pay monthly; studios get ongoing royalties.

### Revenue Model

| Channel | Price Point | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **Standard ticket** | $100 | Same as music spectacles — studio royalties mirror artist royalties |
| **Neural Pass inclusion** | Bundled | Part of the subscriber library; studios receive per-stream or revenue share royalties |

**Pricing rationale:** Studio IP commands the same premium as A-list artist performances. The 20–30% revenue share to rights holders mirrors the artist royalty structure, so the economics are equivalent. Fans of these franchises have demonstrated willingness to pay $100+ for theme park experiences, $200+ for convention tickets, and $1,000+ for collectibles. Standing inside the Dune arena or on the Pelennor Fields is worth $100.

### Strategic Value

- **Massive built-in audiences.** Dune, LOTR, Star Wars, Marvel — these fandoms number in the hundreds of millions.
- **Premium pricing justified.** Fans will pay $100+ to stand inside a scene they've watched dozens of times.
- **Differentiated from theme parks.** Disney/Universal offer rides; vZero offers presence. Not competitive — complementary.
- **Repeatable.** Fans will return for different scenes, different films, different franchises.
- **Tentpole release tie-ins.** Launch a vZero experience alongside a theatrical release (Dune 3, Avatar 3) for cross-promotional impact.

## Vertical 3: Video Game Immersion

### The Opportunity

Video games have larger audiences than film. GTA V sold 200 million copies. Minecraft has 170 million monthly active players. The Legend of Zelda, Mario, Halo, God of War — these are cultural touchstones with fanbases spanning generations.

Gamers have spent thousands of hours in these worlds on screens. vZero offers something entirely new: **standing inside them.**

### The Technical Advantage

Game assets are already real-time 3D — closer to vZero's requirements than film VFX:

- **Environments** are built for real-time rendering (optimized geometry, baked lighting, LOD systems)
- **Characters** are rigged and animated for interactive use
- **Art direction** is established — visual identity, color palettes, iconic locations are defined

The conversion pipeline is straightforward: export game assets → enhance to volumetric fidelity (higher resolution textures, 4DGS character captures or upscaled models) → author for WFS audio and haptic integration.

For stylized games (Mario, Zelda, Minecraft), vZero can offer two modes:
1. **Faithful** — the world as it appears in the game, but in volumetric 3D
2. **Lifelike** — a photorealistic reimagining of the world, as if it were real

|------|----------------|------------|

| **The Legend of Zelda** | Hyrule Field / Temple of Time | The iconic landscape stretches before you. Link rides past on Epona. The Temple of Time rises in the distance. (Faithful or lifelike versions.) |
| **Super Mario** | Peach's Castle / Bob-omb Battlefield | The castle courtyard from Mario 64, rendered in volumetric 3D. Walk through the painting into Bob-omb Battlefield. Experience the world that defined 3D gaming. |

This vertical requires partnerships with:

**Licensing structure options:**

**Why publishers would participate:**

- **Marketing for new releases.** vZero experience launches alongside GTA VII or Zelda sequel.
- **Fan engagement.** Deepens relationship with most dedicated players.
- **Premium positioning.** This is not a mobile port or VR minigame — it's a prestige experience.

**Special opportunity — Valve IP:**

As the founding partner, Valve could provide first-party content at favorable terms:

- **Left 4 Dead** horde moments
- **Team Fortress 2** iconic maps (stylized → lifelike)
- **Counter-Strike** classic maps (de_dust2, de_nuke) as explorable spaces

First-party Valve content would be exclusive to vZero and the Neural Pass — a powerful differentiator.

Standing inside Vice City or Hyrule Field — worlds they've spent hundreds of hours in on a screen — is worth $100 for 20–30 minutes of volumetric presence.

## Open Questions

### Educational
1. **Institutional partnerships:** Which institutions (Smithsonian, British Museum, NASA, National Geographic) should we approach first?

### Cinematic
2. **VFX studio relationships:** DNEG has expressed interest. Who else? Wētā? ILM? Framestore?

3. **Film studio rights:** Which studios are most likely to license IP for immersive experiences? Warner Bros. (Dune, LOTR, Matrix) has the most relevant portfolio.

4. **Cannibalization risk:** Do film studios worry that vZero cannibalizes theme park revenue? Or is this positioned as a different product?

### Gaming
5. **Valve first-party content:** Will Valve provide Half-Life, Portal, and other IP as exclusive first-party content for vZero? At what royalty terms?

6. **Nintendo:** Notoriously protective of IP. Is a partnership realistic, or do we focus on other publishers first?

7. **Rockstar/Take-Two:** GTA and Red Dead are the most photorealistic open worlds. What's the path to a licensing deal?

8. **User-generated content:** Is Minecraft world upload a viable premium feature? What are the technical and rights implications?

### Cross-Vertical
9. **Exclusivity:** Can we secure exclusive volumetric rights to certain franchises? "The only place to stand inside Middle-earth" or "The only place to walk through Vice City."

10. **Content scheduling:** How do we balance venue time across verticals? Music evenings/weekends, educational weekday mornings, cinematic/gaming throughout?
